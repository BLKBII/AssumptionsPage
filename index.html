Our five-year return assumptions for equities and bonds are near post-financial crisis lows. Three overarching points to keep in mind:
  • We expect compressed market returns in the next five years due to stretched valuations and moderate economic growth. Our base case calls for 2% real GDP growth for advanced economies, inflation below central banks’ targets and 4% to 5% growth in emerging markets.
  • We see equities outperforming fixed income in the medium term. Our economic base case and the valuation gap between equities and bonds support the case for strong performance from equities relative to fixed income over the next five years.
  • Our five-year return assumptions are lower than our long-term assumptions due to our views on current valuations and macro-economic conditions. Our 10-year-plus capital market assumptions are based on normalized valuations and interest rate levels, and are driven by five risk premia: equity, duration, inflation, credit and illiquidity.

OUR THINKING ON CAPITAL MARKET RETURN ASSUMPTIONS

Our five-year assumptions can help investors adjust their strategic asset allocation when valuations and macroeconomic conditions substantially deviate from ‘normal’ levels. They incorporate valuation signals and a macroeconomic scenario described in the tables further below. Our assumptions have gradually come down since the financial crisis. The chart below illustrates this from a UK currency perspective.
Our 10-year-plus assumptions reflect ‘equilibrium’ or ‘valuation-neutral’ market conditions that we would expect in the long run. Our approach is based on the capital asset pricing model, which holds that each asset class earns a return equal to the risk-free rate plus a risk premium. For an in-depth discussion of our 10-year-plus assumptions as well as details on our use of BlackRock proxies for some asset classes, see our Long-Term Capital Market Assumptions publication. We detail our thinking on strategic asset allocation in our quarterly Strategic Perspectives publications.
Our Capital Markets Assumptions Group is responsible for formulating the assumptions. The group consists of senior members from the BlackRock Investment Institute, BlackRock Solutions, and BlackRock’s Risk and Quantitative Analysis group, combining experience in portfolio management, economics, central bank policy setting, asset allocation and risk management.

ASSUMPTIONS UNDERLYING LONG-TERM GOVERNMENT BOND RETURNS US EU UK
Long-term inflation (core CPI) We expect central banks to meet their core inflation targets in the long run.
Real rate: Ultra-long maturities We assume that normalised real interest rates on long-term bonds are 2% in the US and EU. In the UK, we assume a lower rate due to structural demand from institutional investors for long-dated government bonds.
Real rate: 10-year maturities We assume that 10-year yields are around 0.25% below ultra-long yields in the US and EU, reflecting an upward-sloping yield curve in which additional return is required for taking more duration risk (a positive term premium). In the UK, we assume a flat nominal yield curve and downward sloping real yield curve beyond 10 years. Therefore, we assume the 10-year UK real rate is higher than the 50-year rate.
Time to reach normalised yield levels We expect eurozone bond yields to reach their normalized long-term level in 10 years’ time given low inflationary pressures. By contrast, we see US and UK bond yields reaching their long-term levels within seven years.
